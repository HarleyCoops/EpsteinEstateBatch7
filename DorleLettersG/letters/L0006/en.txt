We had, I think, 6-8 rowboats and one motorboat. You could already go swimming, too. There was just one big catch: the mosquitoes. Uschi got eaten alive, as did everyone who hadn't experienced an early summer on the lakes before.
There was a guy with us, Heinz, who was fishing, and the blood was just running down his face, no exaggeration. Uschi was still swollen for days afterward and was scratching like crazy. But they had announced on the radio beforehand that people should be careful and on no account go out without protective oil (but that stuff stinks even worse than the burning oil for the cows).
We took a lot of pictures. It was the first time I really snapped away with the new camera, so I mostly focused on landscapes, except for the cover story "Water Battle," which is a twelve-shot sequence of a fight.

Mechi is going to B.C., leaving on July 1st, which will be quite an experience. I'm sure she'll write to you about it herself. She's going to take my Isolette for black and white and hers for color slides. Since I have my vacation at the exact same time, I can't very well give her mine, and besides, if she loses it or something else happens to it, hers is easier to replace. Yesterday, Mechi was offered a great job as a photographer at the television station, $40 a week, but she didn't take it because of B.C. I wouldn't have done that. She could have stopped working a bit earlier next spring and then gone to Europe via B.C. and California. At that job, she would have had every Sunday off and every other weekend from Friday to Tuesday. And it would have certainly been interesting work. She also thought that $40 a week wasn't enough.
