IV
We were so bloody in the face, in our hair, and on our arms and legs from the mosquito bites that we couldn't stand it anymore. The next morning, Siegfried, Heinz, Dagmar, and I set out at exactly 6 o'clock in the rowboat to take photos. The lake is very large and full of countless islands, on which there is wild forest, rocks, and undergrowth. Often, one looks out over the lake from a rock, or sees wild tree roots. On the many pictures that Siegfried, Dagmar, and I took, everything can be seen clearly. At 9 o'clock we drove back to the cabins, had breakfast, played at fishing from the boat in the lake, and also fished. Then Heinz, Siegfried, and I went out again. We had seen a beautiful island that morning, where we wanted to sunbathe. However, that didn't happen, because there were so many people from the club on the lake who kept following us. When they wanted to tow us with their motorboat, Siegfried sprayed them with water. That was the best declaration of war for the ensuing water battle.
