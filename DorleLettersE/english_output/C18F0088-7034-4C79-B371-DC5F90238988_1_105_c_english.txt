Once, a large brown bear almost crossed our path. He was sitting in the grass by the road, and as we drove past, he immediately turned around and trotted into the forest. Too bad, we couldn't photograph him.
In Chicoutimi, we even went to the cinema; we saw the same film as on my birthday, "Cinderella". There's a lot of timber industry everywhere there; the entire hills are full of logs, so many, I've never seen anything like it before. We took many pictures, I'll send them later.
The next day, we drove through Laurentide Park; it's a provincial park of 4000 square miles with 1600 lakes. Hunting is not allowed there, but fishing is, for salmon up to 2 meters long and a type of trout. But you can't stay there long or live there because everything to the left and right of the road is such dense primeval forest that you can't go two meters in. But we still went for a swim. The path is simply cut into the forest; the trees are still lying on the left and right. Once we had to stop because two female moose ran across the road.
