Mont Laurier, July 12, '55

Dear Luti,

Well, half our vacation is already over. It’s been simply wonderful. Every day we've spent 2-3 hours in the water—on the air mattress, that is. We don't do any cooking; we eat cucumbers, canned peas with vinegar and oil, and bread, grapefruit and oranges, and dried apricots. Sleeping in the car is working out great. Here's how we do it: towards evening, we wash up and change at a lake, and when it gets dark, Inge gets into her sleeping bag in the back and we drive to the next town, where we then sleep either by the first houses or at a gas station. Early in the morning, around 5 o'clock, I crawl out of my sleeping bag and drive off before people show up; Inge is usually still asleep in the back. At the next lake we wash up and have breakfast, and then we drive on a ways until we feel like stopping for a swim at midday.

We haven't planned our route at all, and that's the best part. After we left Montreal, we followed the river to Quebec. Quebec is wonderful, an American city with city walls.
