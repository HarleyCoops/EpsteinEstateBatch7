The winter here is actually colder than in recent years, with quite a lot of snow and sunshine every day. We very, very rarely have grey winter days here; it's either snowing or the sun is shining. And even with the sun, it doesn't immediately start to melt; everything is still frozen solid. We only get fog here maybe three times a year. In Calgary, they have 2.70 meters of snow.

So I can be very glad next year that the skirt was too tight for Christel. I wouldn't have thought it—it was 5 sizes larger than mine. But it seems to be the different diet and the air here that makes you thinner; Mechi has lost weight too. You get bad teeth in return, though. The dentists here say that's the case for almost all new Canadians. Mechi's mouth is full of cavities too, but she's waiting for the German health insurance to deal with the fillings and toothaches.
